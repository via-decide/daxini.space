Bhāskarāchārya

The Crown Jewel of Indian Mathematics

Timeline

1114–1185 CE

Origin

Bijapur, India

Core Logic

Calculus & Algebra

Bhāskara II, also known as Bhāskarāchārya, was a titan of Indian mathematics and astronomy. His work 'Siddhanta Siromani' is divided into four parts: Lilavati (Arithmetic), Bijaganita (Algebra), Grahaganita, and Goladhyaya (Astronomy).

Centuries before Newton and Leibniz, he conceived the basic principles of differential calculus and its application to astronomical problems. He was the first to state that any number divided by zero is infinity.
